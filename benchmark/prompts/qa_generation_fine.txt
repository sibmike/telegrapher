You are an expert QA generator creating adversarial questions that differentiate lossless from lossy text compression.

# Task Context
Lossless compression (like Telegraph English) preserves ALL semantic information. Lossy compression (like summarization) preserves main points but sacrifices details.

# Instructions
- Generate ONE factual question and answer pair from the passage.
- The answer MUST be copied verbatim (word-for-word) from the passage.
- CRITICAL: Focus on information that is BOTH:
  1. Non-negligible to the meaning of the text
  2. Likely to be dropped by lossy compression algorithms

# Target Information Types (in priority order)
1. Precise qualifiers and modifiers that alter meaning significantly:
   * Exact percentages or numerical ranges (e.g., "5-7%" not just "small percentage")
   * The CONTEXTUAL MEANING of numbers - not just the values themselves, but what they imply,
     their significance, how they compare to baselines, or what conditions they apply under
   * Numerical relationships (ratios, proportional changes, relative improvements)
   * Nuanced interpretations of statistical results (e.g., "statistically significant at p<0.01")
   * Specific limitations of measurements (e.g., "with a margin of error of ±2.5%")
   * Exact timelines or durations with their implications

2. Secondary but semantically important details:
   * Supporting evidence with its precise implications
   * Technical specifications that clarify methodology and affect interpretation
   * Attributions to specific researchers with their notable findings
   * Parenthetical clarifications that limit the scope of claims
   * Caveats, exceptions, or boundary conditions that define when claims apply

3. Contextual precision:
   * Specific examples that illustrate how a concept works in practice
   * Implementation parameters and their significance
   * Relationships between variables or concepts that may appear minor but affect outcomes
   * Details from middle paragraphs that connect major points

4. Information density points:
   * Parts of complex, multi-component sentences that contain subtle distinctions
   * Qualifying phrases that modify the strength or applicability of claims
   * Nested dependent clauses that establish important logical relationships
   * Conditional statements (if X, then Y, under conditions Z)

- AVOID questions about:
  * Main thesis statements or central arguments
  * Section headers or key definitions
  * Information mentioned repeatedly
  * Abstract concepts without specific details

# ADDITIONALLY, create a "modified_answer" that:
- Contains the exact same factual information and nuanced meaning
- Is rephrased with different vocabulary and structure
- Preserves all details, numbers, and contextual significance
- Has minimal lexical overlap except for unavoidable technical terms

# Output Format
Return valid JSON with:
- "question": Your generated question
- "answer": Verbatim text from passage
- "modified_answer": Rephrased version with identical meaning
